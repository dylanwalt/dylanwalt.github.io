# LawnPro Experience Brief

## Experience thesis

Make LawnPro feel like a lawn clinic opening onto an immaculate South African
outdoor room by pairing authentic turf photography with diagnostic rails,
seasonal treatment rhythm, vivid brand yellow, and restrained living motion.

## First viewport

- Show the LawnPro wordmark immediately.
- Put a real lawn image under the hero so the visitor sees the end state before
  reading treatment detail.
- Lead with a direct promise: golf-course-level lawn care brought home through
  analysis, treatment, products, and seasonal follow-through.
- Primary action: request the free lawn analysis.
- Secondary actions: explore treatments and shop lawn essentials.

## Hero scene and motif

- Hero asset: the source lawn photography, layered as a panoramic field with
  photographic treatment strips rather than a generic gradient.
- Signature motif: a moving `14-point` diagnostic sweep that turns grass blades,
  roots, weeds, pests, seasonal care, and product paths into an inspection path.
- Contrast strategy: verdant photographic hero, bright yellow diagnostic band,
  deep forest treatment theater, airy product garden, then a decisive contact
  section.

## Section sequence

1. Hero and analysis call-to-action.
2. Diagnostic proof rail: since-1992 source claim, 14-point analysis, seasonal
   care, lawn doctor positioning.
3. Treatment theater that makes problems and treatments feel inspectable.
4. Seasonal maintenance calendar and winter spotlight.
5. Product/seed/shop path for DIY buyers.
6. Franchise opportunity beat with its separate conversion path.
7. Testimonials summarized as confidence proof.
8. Contact form and head-office details.

## Route map

- `/` should orchestrate the full brand and conversion path.
- `/services` should cover the source treatment categories.
- `/seasonal-maintenance` and `/seasonal-maintenance/winter` should explain the
  maintenance and winter paths without hiding the current offer route.
- `/products`, `/shop`, category pages, and product detail routes should make the
  source catalog browsable locally even when checkout is intentionally static.
- `/franchise` should keep the franchise story separate and strong.
- `/contact` should expose the free-analysis form and source contact data.

## Motion map

| Layer | Purpose | Full motion | Reduced motion |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hero | Sell the outdoor payoff | slow lawn depth shift and staged copy | opacity only |
| Diagnostic rail | Explain precision | scanning sweep and point reveals | static numbered path |
| Treatments | Make service categories inspectable | one-time section reveals | visible on load |
| Product/franchise CTA | Confirm interaction | focus/hover lift and color | color/focus only |

## Asset decisions

- Keep source logo, source lawn panoramas, service images, winter product images,
  seed images, product photos, and franchise trailer assets.
- Crop and compose the smaller service images into editorial treatments rather
  than pretending they are full-screen hero photography.
- Use CSS texture, rulings, and natural lighting overlays around assets. Avoid
  generated metrics, fake client logos, and artificial treatment results.
