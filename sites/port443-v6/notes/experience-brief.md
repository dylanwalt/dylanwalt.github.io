# Port443 V6 — Experience Brief

## Experience thesis

Make Port443 feel like the **operating system for security evidence and
response in MEA** — a real product with real surfaces, paced like Wiz,
narrated like Tines, and typeset like Linear, with the section rhythm and
conversion architecture borrowed from the Apple product-page reference.

## First viewport

- **Brand signal**: Port443 wordmark + shield in the global nav; H1 is a
  brand-led claim, not a tagline.
- **Hero scene**: off-centre split. Left column (about 50% on desktop):
  eyebrow chip, H1 over two type rows, supporting sub-line, two CTAs
  (`Request a demo`, `See OneView`). Right column: a calm, real-feeling
  OneView control surface — live ring, four framework rows, and an evidence
  log. Not a radar; not a terminal.
- **Conversion path**: `Request a demo` is the primary; `See OneView` jumps
  to the OneView product band. The sticky subnav inherits both.
- **Next-section hint**: a slim proof rail (control attestation, posture,
  automation, regional fit) sits visibly below the fold.

## Section sequence (the rebuild's beats)

1. **Hero** — type-led claim + OneView control surface.
2. **Proof rail** — compact evidence rail (control attestation, posture,
   automation, regional fit).
3. **Highlights reel** — five horizontal cards that each link to a deeper
   section.
4. **Control loop** — scroll-pinned, three-state reveal of `Observe →
   Remediate → Verify`. This is the signature interaction. (Tines anchor.)
5. **OneView band** — the dashboard surface shown larger, with framework
   alignment, evidence rows, and board-ready reporting. Light-ivory band.
6. **Regional presence** — the `about-1.jpg` image with a MEA operating
   statement; no invented coverage logos.
7. **Frameworks rail** — single calm row naming PCI-DSS 4.0, NIST CSF 2.0,
   CIS Controls v8, and vendor best practice.
8. **Insights** — LinkedIn posts and press cards, real URLs only.
9. **Contact stage** — repeat `Request a demo` with the form and a short
   path to `/request-a-demo/`.
10. **Footer** — site map + lightweight trust notes.

## Signature interaction

The Control loop section pins for three viewport heights; a left-aligned
diagram builds out across the three pinned states (`Observe → Remediate →
Verify`), while the right-aligned narrative column scrubs through three
matched copy beats and three matched product-surface snippets. Under
`prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` the pin is released and the three states
render as a static three-up grid with the full diagram drawn.

## Three anchor references this experience responds to

1. **Wiz** — hero, color discipline, section banding, and sticky subnav.
2. **Tines** — the scroll-pinned Loop signature interaction.
3. **Linear** — type scale, restraint, calm leading, and editorial pacing.

## Assets

- Keep `hero-bg.jpg` and `about-1.jpg` for the regional band only (not the
  hero — the hero is product-led, not photography-led).

- Compose the OneView surface from primitives; no invented screenshots.
- No invented customer logos or numeric metrics.

## Reduced-motion strategy

- The Control loop pin is released; the three states render as a three-up
  grid.
- The ring and counter render their final state.
- All ambient motion (gradient drift, sparklines) becomes static.
- All text content remains identical and fully reachable by keyboard.
